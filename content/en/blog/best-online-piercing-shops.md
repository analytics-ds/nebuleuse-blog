## Why the choice of shop is decisive

Buying an ear piercing online is not just an aesthetic choice. A **piercing** is a device inserted into the skin, in prolonged contact with an open wound during the first weeks, then with the dermis for years. Material quality directly determines the risk of allergic reaction, especially for helix, tragus and conch piercings, which heal slowly.

The Association of Professional Piercers, a reference body, recommends only ASTM F136 implant-grade titanium, solid gold of at least 14 carats without nickel, or medical glass for a healing piercing. **Gold-plated** items and low-grade surgical steel contain releasable nickel, the leading cause of contact dermatitis according to public Inserm data.

The **best online shops to buy ear piercings** therefore differ mainly on material, origin and product sheet transparency. Price, shipping speed and customer service come next.

### Selection criteria to compare

- Material standard (ASTM F136 for titanium, karat rating for gold)
- Manufacturing origin (Europe, Asia, United States)
- Catalogue depth by piercing type (helix, tragus, conch, lobe, daith)
- Return policy and warranty
- Shipping fees and lead times to mainland France

## How to choose an online piercing shop

Five decision criteria allow a quick choice between brands. The first is the material: ASTM F136 implant-grade titanium or solid gold of at least 14 carats for a healing piercing, with no exception. The second is the manufacturing origin: a French or European shop offers better traceability and better recourse in case of dispute. The third is catalogue depth in the targeted piercing type. The fourth is product sheet transparency (material standard stated, macro photos, dimensions). The fifth is the return policy, ideally 14 to 30 days in France.

### Mistakes to avoid

1. Buying gold-plated for a fresh piercing. Plating wears off quickly and releases the base metal, usually brass, a source of irritation.
2. Ignoring the material standard. A product sold as "titanium" without ASTM F136 specification may contain non-medical alloys.
3. Confusing jewelry karats (gold rating) and lapidary carats (stone weight). 14-carat gold means an alloy at 58.5 percent pure gold.
4. Buying too small. For a healing helix or tragus, plan a bar length 2 to 3 millimetres greater than the strict requirement to handle initial swelling.
5. Neglecting the return policy. Piercings are hygiene items often non-returnable once opened, check the conditions before purchase.
