## What is body jewelry

Body jewelry refers to any piece worn on the body outside the face, wrists and fingers. This category covers five historical families, recently enriched by contemporary innovations like bust jewelry and delicate harnesses.

Long associated with beach or festival codes, body jewelry has moved past these imaginaries to join the daily silhouette. The waist chain goes under a sweater, the anklet pairs with sneakers, back jewelry dresses up a simple top.

## The main families

### Waist chains

The signature piece of the season. Worn on bare skin, swimwear or flowy dress, it redraws the waistline and balances the silhouette with a horizontal line.

### Anklets

The anklet asserts itself in fine multi-strand versions or single models with charms. Symmetrical wear (both ankles) has returned this year after a long absence.

### Back jewelry

The couture piece by excellence. Only wears with backless dresses, low-back cuts or transparent bodysuits. Guaranteed effect for evenings.

### Harnesses

Minimalist harnesses, with fine chains, worn under or over a garment. A modern and soft version of a piece long tied to fetish aesthetics.

### Arm cuffs

The armlet wears above the elbow. Recent trend, in fine or sculptural versions.

## Choosing for your body type

Every body type can wear body jewelry, as long as volume and length match. The classic mistake is following a trend without adapting proportions.

### Slim silhouette

Favor fine tight-link chains, multi-strand for volume. Avoid massive pieces that crush the silhouette.

### Curvy silhouette

Go for wide-link chains, pendant-heavy models, premium materials (solid gold, rhodium silver). Fine pieces get lost, contrast is needed.

### Athletic silhouette

Charm chains, cut-out back jewelry, structured harnesses work very well. Play with anchor points to soften angles.

## Metals for body jewelry

Body jewelry faces more stress than head jewelry: sweat, friction against clothing, chlorinated or salt water exposure. Metal choice is critical.

| Metal | Sea tolerance | Sweat tolerance | Lifespan | Budget |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 14k solid gold | Perfect | Perfect | 15+ years | High |
| 18k solid gold | Perfect | Perfect | 20+ years | Very high |
| 3 micron vermeil | Good | Medium | 3 to 5 years | Medium |
| Classic plated gold | Poor | Poor | 6 months to 2 years | Low |
| Implant titanium | Perfect | Perfect | 15+ years | Medium to high |
| 316L stainless steel | Good | Good | 5 to 10 years | Low to medium |

## Getting the right size

Size is the factor that determines wearing success. Too short creates an ugly mark, too long becomes cumbersome.

### Waist chain

Measure around the desired height (low hip, natural waist, under the bust). Add 5 to 10 cm for a light drape, or 15 to 20 cm for a loose belt effect on the opposite hip.

### Anklet

Measure just above the ankle bone. Add 1 to 2 cm for comfortable wear without sliding.

### Back jewelry

Length is calculated by the dress back cut. Plan a chain ending 2 to 3 cm above the deepest point of the cut.

## Composing with other jewelry

Body jewelry never stands alone. It dialogues with [trendy earrings 2026](/en/blog/trendy-earrings-2026/) and neck pieces to form a complete silhouette.

Three coherence rules:

1. **One dominant metal**: yellow gold, silver or rose, pick one and stick with it.
2. **One statement piece maximum**: if the waist chain is bold, earrings stay minimal.
3. **One focal point**: the eye needs a center of gravity, either up (ears, neck) or down (waist, ankle).

## Care and durability

Body jewelry takes more. Rinse with clear water after each wear, full drying before storage in a soft pouch. Solid gold pieces can pass in a washing machine in a delicate net with no issue.

Perfumes attack plating and dull porous stones. Rule of thumb: perfume first, dress, then jewelry last.
