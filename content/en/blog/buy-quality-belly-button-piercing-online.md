## Belly button piercing and nickel allergy: why the material changes everything

Nickel is the leading cause of allergic contact dermatitis in Europe. According to the EDEN study published in *Contact Dermatitis* (2018, N = 3,119 subjects in 5 European countries), **14.5 percent of the general population is sensitized to nickel**. In women, the figure rises further: the odds ratio reaches 5.19 (95 percent CI: 3.99-6.74). The link with piercings is direct and documented.

A meta-analysis by Spreckelsen et al. (*Contact Dermatitis*, 2025) covering 5,333 individuals confirms that the risk of nickel allergy is **multiplied by 5.9 in piercing wearers**. More worrying: 24.7 percent of piercing jewelry sold in Europe exceeds the nickel release limits set by the EN1811 standard.

In practice, a 316L surgical steel belly button piercing contains between 10 percent and 14 percent nickel in its alloy. Even though release is normally regulated, the pierced area is an open wound that increases allergen penetration into the dermis. **ASTM F136 titanium** (Ti-6Al-4V ELI) solves this problem: it contains no nickel, is biologically inert and is used in implant surgery (hip prostheses, dental implants). ### The 3 criteria to buy a belly button piercing without allergy risk

- **Titanium standard**: require ASTM F136 (implant grade). "Titanium" without standard precision guarantees nothing.
- **Threading type**: internal threading or threadless systems prevent the screw thread from contacting pierced skin, reducing irritation and bacterial risk.
- **Traceability**: prefer shops that explicitly state the standard, material origin and first-insertion compatibility.

## How to choose a quality belly button piercing online without allergy risk

### Mistakes to avoid

1. **Buying a "titanium" piercing without checking the standard**. The mention "titanium" alone does not guarantee ASTM F136 grade. Some sellers use G5 titanium (industrial grade) or do not specify the alloy.
2. **Choosing external threading for a first insertion**. The outer screw thread scrapes the piercing channel on insertion, promoting irritation and infection.
3. **Changing the jewelry too early**. A belly button piercing heals in 6 to 12 months. Removing the first jewelry before complete healing causes micro-lesions and delays healing.
4. **Relying on price alone**. An ASTM F136 titanium belly button piercing costs between 15 € and 39 €. Below 10 €, it is almost always uncertified steel or titanium.

### Pre-purchase checklist

- Does the product page mention "ASTM F136" or "implant grade titanium"?
- Is the jewelry guaranteed nickel-free?
- Is the closure system threadless or internally threaded?
- Does the shop offer return or refund?
- Is the jewelry compatible with first insertion (autoclave sterilizable)?
